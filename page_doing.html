    • Subtext
      January 2024
      Multimodal Interface, Programming, AI Application
      Tools: Touchdesigner, Python, OpenAI (Whisper, ChatGPT), StableDiffusion, Mediapipe
      Subtext was a multimodal AI tool designed to help users in spoken situations. Speech and gesture input is transformed into text and image output in the form of live subtitles, summaries and lists, and visuals that relate to what is being said. Envisioned for use in classrooms, presentations, and meetings to be assistive to all, and particularly useful to those with hearing and attentional deficits.
    • Den Underliga Havsmattan
      October 2023
      Interaction Design, 3D Modelling, Video Production
      Tools: Blender, Da Vinci Resolve
      "Den Underliga Havsmattan" (The Strange Sea Mat) was a physical interface concept to encourage body movement during computer usage through novelty and play, and without sacrificing comfort.

      The name of this design is inspired by a poem by Edith Sodergran.
      Designed for a physical interaction design course.

    • Syncing
      Performance, Sonification, Programming
      May 2023
      Tools: SuperCollider, Soundgrain, NGIMU Motion Sensor
      Collaborator: Helena Linder Miñambres
      “Syncing” is a sound and dance collaborative performance composed of sonifications of climate change data where dancer/choreographers use their bodies as instruments, generating sound through movement. By giving their actions a voice, which grows more powerful as the dancers reach synchronisation, Helena and I hoped to inspire in our audience a sense of agency, opportunity for cooperation and shared resilience in the face of catastrophes.

      Performed in Reaktorhallen at KTH as part of Navet’s 4-day endeavour to depict climate change data through sound and dance, in collaboration with SKH Dance and KMH.
    • A Sound Forest Time Machine
      April 2023
      Interactive installation, Programming, Sound Design
      Tools: SuperCollider, Stable Diffusion
      Collaborator: Helena Linder Miñambres
      Sound Forest Time Machine is an interactive soundscape installation produced using Supercollider (sclang) and a prototype of the Sound Forest digital musical instrument (DMI), a permanent installation at Scenkonst Museet (Performing Arts Museum), Stockholm.

      Produced for a Sound in Interaction course.

      Concept art for this project was generated using Stable Diffusion.

    • Flow
      May 2021
      Video Production, VFX
      Tools: Adobe Premiere Pro, Adobe After Effects
      "Flow" is a music video created to study the work of artist Kevin McGloughlin. Surrealist glitch effects and transitions were explored in the style of his work with musician Max Cooper, for example Resynthesis and Lovesong.

      Produced for a video production course remotely during COVID using only freely available stock footage.
